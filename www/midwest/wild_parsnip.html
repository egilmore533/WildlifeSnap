Wild Parsnip



FACTS

Plant secretes a severely irritating sap that absorbs into the skin on contact. Irritated, blistered, and peeling skin follows elongated streaks and spots that mimic the leaves and stems that the victim came in contact with Irritations generally will not appear until the area is exposed to direct light. Repeated and long exposure to the sap will result in small chemical burns. Burns will appear similar to poison ivy rashes



SAFETY

Wear protective clothing (i.e. long sleeved shirts, goggles, rubber gloves, bootes, etc.) when working in areas with Wild Parsnip. If victim comes in contact with plant, immediately rinse with cool water. Treat blisters by covering with bandages; do not remove if blisters rupture. Keep area of exposure clean and apply antibiotic cream. Contact your municipality to arrange for removal of plants to avoid other exposures
