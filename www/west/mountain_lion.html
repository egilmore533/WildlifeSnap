Cougar



FACTS

Can bound up to 40 feet, jump 15 feet up a tree, climb a 12 foot fence, and sprint up to 50 mph. Mountain lions are typically solitary and extremely territorial. They will mark their territories by clawing trees and marking trees, leaf piles, or grass patches. Female mountain lions will periodically move their kittens from den to den in order to protect them from predators and male lions



SAFETY

Risk of mountain lion attack to human is relatively small. The lions tend to avoid people and populated areas. Mountain lions are most active at dusk and dawn, so avoid walking alone with small children and pets at these times. Never approach or corner a mountain lionIf you do encounter a mountain lion, DO NOT RUN. Make yourself look bigger by opening your coat or raising your arms. Throw an item at the lion if necessary. Do NOT act timid or play dead.If attacked, fight back. The lion will usually be driven away if given the time and space to leave.
